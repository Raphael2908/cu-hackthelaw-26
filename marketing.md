# Marketing / GTM

## What it is
A supervision cockpit for human + AI legal teams. It delegates work under partner approval, then
triages completed AI output by a risk signal, surfaces **checkable flags** (never verdicts), and
records defensible, signed sign-off — so a supervising partner stays accountable at scale.

## The wedge
Every firm adopting legal AI hits the same wall: *a human cannot review every AI output but remains
legally responsible for all of it.* The market's reflex — add a "checker AI" that scores outputs —
just relocates the trust problem to a new black box ("who watches the watchmen?"). Our answer is
structurally different: we surface claims a human can verify in seconds and we sample the rest like
a financial audit. That is the difference between *looks supervised* and *is supervisable*.

## ICP (first users)
- **Primary:** supervising partners and senior associates in BigLaw / international firms (the
  Clifford Chance archetype) who are piloting AI for document review and are personally on the hook.
- **Buyer / champion:** the firm's innovation / legal-engineering / risk-and-compliance function,
  which needs a defensible audit trail to greenlight AI use at all.

## Why now
Regulators and courts are starting to ask *how* AI output was supervised, not just whether AI was
used. "We had a checker model" is not a defence; "here is the signed record of who decided what, on
what checkable evidence, with a sampled audit of the auto-cleared work" is.

## Positioning vs alternatives
- **vs raw LLM review tools:** they produce output; we make output *supervisable* and *accountable*.
- **vs checker-AI / scoring products:** they emit a verdict to trust; we emit flags to verify, and
  we never auto-approve.
- **vs do-nothing (manual review):** doesn't scale; the partner becomes the bottleneck or rubber-stamps.

## First 10 users (motion)
1. Demo to one innovation team at a target firm; run their real review process against a planted-defect
   corpus so the signals visibly catch what manual review would miss under time pressure.
2. Land a single practice group (e.g. finance/transactional) as a design partner; tune severity
   labels to their process doc and standards.
3. Expand by task type within the firm once the audit trail clears risk/compliance.

## The one-liner for the slide
> An audit-grade supervision layer for legal AI: checkable flags, randomly sampled clearance, and a
> signed record — so the partner stays accountable without reading every output.
